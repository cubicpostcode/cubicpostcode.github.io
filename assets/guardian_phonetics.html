Feature: How the IPA Became a Universal Alphabet

The IPA grew out of a practical classroom need: show exact pronunciation independent of spelling. Its design principles include one‚Äësymbol‚Äëper‚Äësound, diacritics for fine detail, and a clear separation between phones (actual sounds) and phonemes (contrastive categories).
