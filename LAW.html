"Mad in America"] and Scientologists of the International Church of
Scientology,

I hope this letter finds you well. My name is Cubic, and I am writing to
you to share my deeply personal and ongoing struggle with being coerced
into psychiatric treatment and my fervent desire to free myself from the
oppressive cycle of forced consultations and imposed treatments.

For much of my life, I have been subjected to a regimen of psychiatric
drugs, often against my will. This enforced medicalization has been a
significant source of distressful ongoing situation and set of experiences
that I can only describe as torture or one of your worst nightmares and a
source of continuous frustration for me. The constant pressure to attend
consultations with psychiatrists, who frequently impose their treatment
plans without genuinely considering my perspective or well-being and proper
social housing and adequate social benefits, has been overwhelmingly
challenging.

Since the early stages of my academic career in the 1990s, and more
formally since 2005, I have sought solace and support in my work as an
independent advocate and through my involvement with the International
Church of Scientology. The principles and community I have found there have
been instrumental in helping me navigate my journey towards mental health
and autonomy. Despite these positive influences, I still face considerable
obstacles in breaking free from the constraints of enforced psychiatric
treatment.

Given your esteemed work with the United Nations and your advocacy for
human rights within the mental health system, I am reaching out to you in
the hope of establishing a connection. Your expertise and dedication to
reforming mental health practices align closely with my own experiences and
aspirations. I am particularly interested in exploring any possible legal
avenues that might help me and others in similar situations to achieve
greater freedom and self-determination.

I would be immensely grateful for any guidance, support, or connections you
could offer as I continue to advocate for my rights and seek to live a life
free from coercive psychiatric interventions. Your work at Mad in America
and at the international level inspires me, and I am eager to collaborate
or learn from your experiences to further our shared goals.

Thank you very much for your time and consideration. I look forward to the
possibility of connecting with you and working together towards a more just
and humane approach to mental health.
