The imaginary axis out of the North Pole always aims toward Polaris (the brightest star in Ursa Minor, also called the North Star). The direction of the axis does not change much as the Earth revolves around the Sun. Seasons arise because the Earth’s axis is not perpendicular to the plane containing the Earth’s orbit. The axis makes an angle of 66.5° with that plane, or 23.5° to the perpendicular to it. Any questions?
