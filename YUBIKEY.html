Using a Yubikey to Secure Your Gmail Account

Step 1: Register Your Yubikey with Your Google Account

Insert Yubikey: Plug your Yubikey into a USB port on your computer.

Sign In to Google Account:

  • Open a web browser and go to myaccount.google.com/security.
  • Sign in to your Google account.

Security Settings:

  • In the "Signing in to Google" section, find and click on "2-Step Verification".
  • If prompted, sign in again.

Add Security Key:

  • Scroll down to "Add more second steps to verify itâ€™s you" and find "Security Key".
  • Click on "Add Security Key" and follow the on-screen instructions.
  • When prompted, insert your Yubikey (if it's not already inserted) and tap it when the light flashes.

Confirm and Name Your Key:

  • Give your security key a name (e.g., "Yubikey").
  • Complete the process by following the remaining prompts.

Step 2: Using Your Yubikey

Sign In:

  • Go to the Google login page and enter your email and password as usual.
  • When prompted for your second factor of authentication, insert your Yubikey and tap it.

Access via Mobile:

  • If you're accessing Gmail on your phone and your phone supports USB-OTG, you can use a USB-C or micro-USB adapter to connect your Yubikey.
  • Alternatively, if your Yubikey has NFC, you can tap it on the back of your phone.

Best Practices for Using Yubikey in Public Places:

Keep It Secure:

  • Always keep your Yubikey with you. Attach it to your keychain or store it in a safe place.
  • Consider using a cover for your Yubikey to protect it from physical damage.

Avoid Public USB Ports:

  • Avoid using public USB ports to insert your Yubikey, as these could be compromised (a practice known as "juice jacking").
  • Use your own USB adapter or portable charger if necessary.

Privacy:

  • When using your Yubikey in public, shield your screen to prevent shoulder surfing.
  • Avoid logging into sensitive accounts when others are nearby or watching.

Backup Options:

  • Set up a backup security key or alternative 2-step verification methods (like Google Authenticator or backup codes) in case you lose your Yubikey.

Regular Checks:

  • Periodically check your security settings to ensure your Yubikey is still registered and active.
  • Review your account activity for any unauthorized access.

Additional Tips:

Using Yubikey with Mobile Devices:

  • USB-OTG Support: Ensure your mobile device supports USB-OTG (On-The-Go) to connect your Yubikey via an adapter.
  • NFC Capability: If your Yubikey supports NFC, simply tap it against your phone to authenticate.
  • Google Authenticator: As a backup, install the Google Authenticator app on your phone.

Label Your Keys: If you have multiple Yubikeys, label them to differentiate easily.

Firmware Updates: Check for firmware updates for your Yubikey and apply them as necessary.

Travel: When traveling, consider carrying a backup Yubikey stored separately from your primary one.
