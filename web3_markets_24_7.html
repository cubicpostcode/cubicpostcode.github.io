24/7 Web3 Markets — Abolishing Traditional Brokerages (Q&A)

Question

“Imagine you ask Ivan (the Swedish YouTuber from IvanOnTech) and Fernando Ulrich for a 10-step, step-by-step tutorial to abolish stock-market brokerages and mediators/arbitrators/middlemen—making markets much more web3 and blockchain-native and operating 24/7. What would they say?”

Answer — 10 Steps to Broker-less, 24/7 Web3 Markets

  1. Choose the lawful wrapper for on-chain equities. Use a tokenized-security template that encodes shareholder rights (voting, dividends, disclosures) and maps directly to company/securities law so the token is the cap table.
  2. Ship a reusable identity layer. After KYC/AML, issue verifiable credentials (DIDs). Enforce transfer rules via contract whitelists; use zero-knowledge proofs to keep compliance private when possible.
  3. Put issuance and the cap table on-chain. Mint programmable shares (e.g., ERC-1400/restricted ERC-20). Corporate actions—splits, buybacks, dividends—become callable functions with immutable logs.
  4. Make the venue code, not a company. Deploy an on-chain exchange using CLOB/AMM hybrid with batch auctions to blunt MEV. Open APIs so any party can run a front end; core logic lives in audited contracts.
  5. Atomic settlement (DvP) by default. Cash leg in regulated stablecoins (or CBDCs when available). T+0 finality: if payment clears, shares move in the same transaction.
  6. Scale with rollups & account abstraction. Trade on a zk/optimistic rollup; proofs commit to L1. Smart wallets enable social recovery; institutions can opt for MPC custodians.
  7. Trustable data: oracles & disclosures. Use decentralized oracles for reference prices, FX, and events. Store issuer filings on IPFS/Arweave with on-chain hashes and scheduled update requirements.
  8. Program compliance & risk controls. Policy contracts define transfer restrictions, circuit-breakers, and kill-switches; governed by tokenholders and/or regulators. On-chain margining and real-time collateral checks; market-maker proof-of-reserves.
  9. Unbundle the “broker” into value-adding roles. Wallet providers focus on UX/custody; liquidity providers run permissioned MM contracts; compliance agents act as attestors issuing/revoking credentials—competing on speed, cost, and transparency.
  10. Migrate liquidity, then switch to 24/7. Start with blue-chip listings and transparent maker rebates. Run continuous trading with time-boxed auctions around major disclosures. Offer one-click tokenization/swap of legacy shares; gradually sunset old rails.

Mini-Checklist (who does what)

  • Issuers: adopt tokenized-equity template; commit to on-chain disclosures.
  • Investors: obtain reusable KYC credential; use smart wallets.
  • Regulators: co-govern policy contracts; run read-only nodes.
  • Dev/Infra (Ivan-style): harden rollups, wallets, audits; suppress MEV with auctions.
  • Econ/Policy (Fernando-style): insist on sound collateral (quality stablecoins), clear property rights, open competition among front ends and LPs.

Expected Outcomes

  • No custodial middleman by default; user-controlled keys or chosen custody.
  • No separate clearing house; settlement is a function call (atomic DvP).
  • Markets run 24/7; risk controls are transparent and programmatic.
Caveats: Securities laws still apply. Favor jurisdictions recognizing tokenized instruments; use regulated stablecoins (or CBDCs) on the cash leg. This is market-structure design, not investment advice.
